About the Project

Death Bloom is a short puzzle horror game created in Unreal Engine 5 by the University of Texas at Dallas' Spring 2023 Game Lab. This project was created by a team of 45+ people. I was the Lead Sound Designer and was in charge of 3 main sound design students (part of the project for the entire semester) plus 13 "contract" sound design students (worked on the project for around half of the semester).

My role for this project included establishing audio standards, determining what sounds needed to be present, approving or requesting changes for submitted sounds, ensuring that the sounds fit with what designers and artists had created, and implementing those sounds and requests into engine. This role required a lot of communication - not only between the sound team members, but also between deparatments - to set deadlines and expectations for everyone involved.

For me, this project involved mostly working in engine to implement sounds and communication with other deparatments. From this project, I gained a lot of experience using Unreal Engine 5's audio tools. This includes audio volumes, sound cues, and most fun, Metasounds. As I did most of the sound implementation, I also did a lot of work using Unreal's blueprint system.

Creating this MetaSound was surprisingly challenging. When the player first gets the lantern, there is no magical ambience. The magical ambience is controlled by two inputs, isMagical and isLevel3. This is because the magical ambience is two parts: the regular magic and the "moon" magic which is important to the narrative of the game. These inputs are changed in the lantern blueprint as well as the level blueprint. However, before the lantern becomes magical, I want the lantern noise loop to have some variation. I accomplished this by having two wave players, one to play the regular loop and the second to play a pitch shifted loop. I have two wave players so that the change in frequency is not abrput, but interps between the shifted loop and the regular loop.

This MetaSound controls the ambience for level 2. Of the three levels in the game, this one has the most variation in ambience. There are 5 parts to it: the room tone, low drone, medium drone, intense drone, and the "spooky zone". I am able to balance these relative to each other in the MetaSound as the game is playing. Additionally, I learned a lot about generating sounds in engine when making the "spooky zone" ambience. The ambience samples a very small part of an asset used in a different part of the level to create a rhythmic, looping sound. In this demo, the audio asset for the "Intense Drone" has not yet been added. Unlike other assets in the game, this is also a stereo asset as I wanted it to play non-diegetically.

In Level 1, the player crafts something by pouring liquids. I wanted the sound to increase in pitch as the player added more liquid to the bucket to mimic real life. The player can pour at most two liquids into the bucket. To mimic this, I created this MetaSound to flip between the two assets I wanted to play. This was easier than doing it in a blueprint or another method as I could easily tie the MetaSound to the liquid pouring animation. Unfortunately, the video doesn't showcase the MetaSound acutally working as Unreal cannot simulate that, but it does work in the final build.
